What are Near-Earth Objects (NEOs)?

Near-Earth Objects are asteroids and comets with orbits that bring them within 195 million kilometers of the Sun, meaning they can pass relatively close to Earth's orbit.

Size Classification

  • Meteoroids: < 1 meter (burn up in atmosphere)
  • Small Asteroids: 1-140 meters (regional damage)
  • Large Asteroids: 140m-1km (continental effects)
  • Global Killers: > 1km (mass extinction events)

Orbital Mechanics

Space objects follow elliptical orbits governed by gravity. Key parameters include:

  • Semi-major axis: Average distance from Sun
  • Eccentricity: How elliptical the orbit is
  • Inclination: Tilt relative to Earth's orbit
  • Orbital period: Time to complete one orbit

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How likely is an asteroid impact?

Small impacts (1-10m) happen monthly. City-destroying impacts (140m+) occur every 10,000-100,000 years. Civilization-ending impacts (1km+) happen every few million years.

How do we detect asteroids?

Ground-based telescopes like Catalina Sky Survey and LINEAR scan the sky nightly. Space-based missions like NEOWISE use infrared detection. Over 90% of kilometer-sized NEOs have been found.

What was the Tunguska event?

In 1908, an asteroid or comet ~60m across exploded over Siberia, flattening 2,000 km¬≤ of forest. It released energy equivalent to 10-15 megatons of TNT but caused no direct human casualties due to the remote location.

Could we stop a "planet killer" asteroid?

With enough warning time (years to decades), yes. Multiple spacecraft could be sent to gradually deflect the asteroid. The key is early detection - we need at least 5-10 years for large objects.

What happened to the dinosaurs?

66 million years ago, a 10-15km asteroid hit the Yucatan Peninsula, creating the Chicxulub crater. The impact caused global fires, tsunamis, and a "nuclear winter" that led to the extinction of non-avian dinosaurs.

How accurate are impact predictions?

For well-observed asteroids, orbital predictions are very accurate over decades. However, small forces like solar radiation pressure and gravitational perturbations can cause uncertainty over longer periods.
